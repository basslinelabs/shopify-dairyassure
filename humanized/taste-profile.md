# Taste Profile
Last updated: 2026-03-15
Calibration sessions: 1

## Text Preferences
- Approach preference: minimal
- Humor tolerance: none to subtle (never tryhard)
- Formality level: conversational but not chatty
- Specificity appetite: moderate (real facts over stories)
- Fragment tolerance: high (loves short fragments as full statements)
- Run-on sentences: yes, use intentionally. They create natural momentum and feel like real speech.
- Cultural references: avoids
- Rhetorical questions: avoids ("What if you could..." is a red flag)
- Metaphors/analogies: avoids in copy (state things plainly)
- Personality injection: only when context calls for it, never in informational content
- CTA style: direct and conventional over clever

## Punctuation & Formatting
- Em dashes: NEVER USE. Instant AI tell. Use periods, commas, or restructure instead.
- Prefers periods and short sentences over long clauses joined by punctuation
- Sentence length: short. Trusts the reader to fill gaps.

## Image Preferences
- Style preference: documentary / authentic mess
- Imperfection tolerance: high (messy counters, torn packets, real life context)
- Lighting preference: natural only (window light, real environments)
- Composition preference: candid, un-styled, anti-flat-lay
- Props: real and incidental (phone, keys, half-eaten food) not arranged

## Instant-Fail Tells (user's personal red flags)
1. Em dashes anywhere in copy
2. "What if you could..." rhetorical openers
3. Forced humor or puns
4. Metaphors explaining simple concepts ("Think of it like...")
5. Overly curated/styled product photography
6. Tricolon repetition: "No X, no Y, no Z" parallel structures
7. "No X, no Y, just Z" setup-setup-pivot construction

## Copy Selection Patterns (from calibration)
- Round 1 (hero headline): Chose shortest, most direct. "Eat the cheese."
- Round 2 (abandoned cart subject): Chose minimal. "Still thinking about it?"
- Round 3 (FAQ answer): Chose most informational, zero personality. Plain facts.
- Round 4 (Instagram caption): Chose shortest. "Pizza night. No compromises."
- Round 5 (social proof): Chose real statistic over testimonial or tagline.
- Round 6 (welcome email): Chose informational, efficient, no fluff.
- Round 7 (image direction): Chose messy real kitchen over styled or editorial.
- Round 8 (paid ad): Chose conventional direct ad copy over minimalist or rhetorical.

## Core Principles (distilled)
1. Say less. Trust the reader.
2. State the problem plainly. State the solution plainly.
3. No cleverness for its own sake.
4. Informational contexts get zero personality injection.
5. Real facts and numbers over stories and metaphors.
6. Authenticity = messiness, not curation.
7. The best copy sounds like it took 10 seconds to write (even if it didn't).

## User-Reported Frameworks
- Em dashes are an automatic AI signal. If copy has em dashes, it reads as AI-generated.
