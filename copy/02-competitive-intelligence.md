## Website Copy Patterns

```
WEBSITE COPY PATTERNS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HERO HEADLINE PATTERNS
  Pattern 1: "Outcome + Without the Downside"
    Examples: "Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama" (DairyAssure — already uses
              this), "Real milk with no discomfort" (Lactaid),
              "Enjoy gluten and dairy without a bloated, upset stomach" (Hilma)
    Why it works: Names both the desire and the fear in one line
    Frequency: 6 of 13 brands use this pattern

  Pattern 2: "Category Disruption / Generational Contrast"
    Examples: "Not Your Grandma's Fiber" (Bellway), "More Than A Greens
              Powder" (AG1), "The evolution of probiotics" (Seed)
    Why it works: Repositions a stale category as modern/exciting
    Frequency: 4 of 13 brands

  Pattern 3: "Single-Action Simplification"
    Examples: "One Scoop a Day" (AG1), "Three steps. Zero effort."
              (DairyAssure homepage)
    Why it works: Reduces complexity anxiety; makes the product feel effortless
    Frequency: 3 of 13 brands

PDP DESCRIPTION PATTERNS
  Pattern 1: "Problem → Failed Solutions → This Product → Proof"
    Used by: Enzymedica, Arrae, Hilma, AG1
    Structure: Name the discomfort → acknowledge what people have tried →
               introduce the product → clinical/review proof

  Pattern 2: "Permission + Mechanism"
    Used by: Lactaid, Hilma, Bellway
    Structure: "Enjoy [food] without [problem]" → here's how it works

  Pattern 3: "Education-First"
    Used by: Seed, Seeking Health
    Structure: Teach the biology → position product as the logical conclusion

CTA PATTERNS
  What converts: Specific > generic
    Strong: "TRY DAIRYASSURE TODAY", "Subscribe now to get your FREE
            Welcome Kit" (AG1), "Try Bloat" (Arrae)
    Weak: "Shop Now", "Add to Cart", "Where to Buy"
  Subscription-first: AG1, Seed default to subscription CTA
  Quiz/assessment: Hims, Seeking Health use quiz as low-commitment entry

SOCIAL PROOF PATTERNS
  Placement: Best brands put social proof in 3 locations:
    1. Hero area (star rating / review count / "trusted by X")
    2. Near CTA / buy button (badges, guarantee)
    3. Dedicated section mid-page (testimonials, reviews)
  Type hierarchy (most to least effective in this category):
    1. Clinical trial results (Seed, AG1, Arrae — "reduces bloating by 86%")
    2. Doctor/expert endorsement (Seeking Health, Hilma, AG1 + Huberman)
    3. Customer review volume ("127 reviews", "500K+ customers")
    4. Press/retail presence (Arrae at Erewhon, Bellway at Target)
    5. Certifications (Kosher, Non-GMO, Clean Label)
  DairyAssure gap: Has #3 and #5. Missing #1, #2, and #4.

OBJECTION HANDLING PATTERNS
  Top objections in lactase/digestive supplement category:
    1. "Does it actually work?" → Clinical data, specific % claims, timelines
    2. "What's in it?" → Single ingredient, clean label, allergen-free
    3. "Is it worth the price?" → Daily cost reframe, vs-supplements chart
    4. "Is it inconvenient?" → Stick packs, no planning, works instantly
    5. "Is it safe for my situation?" → Age ranges, doctor-recommended, FAQ
  How winners address them:
    - Inline near CTA (Ridge guarantee, AG1 price reframe)
    - Comparison table (AG1 vs supplement cabinet, DairyAssure vs pills)
    - FAQ accordion (universal — every brand does this)
    - Trust badge bar near buy button (certifications, guarantees)

TRUST SIGNAL PATTERNS
  Table stakes (every brand):
    - Money-back guarantee (30-90 days)
    - Non-GMO / allergen-free badges
    - Made in USA
  Differentiators (only the best):
    - Clinical trial data (Seed, AG1, Arrae)
    - Doctor/expert formulation (Seeking Health, Hilma)
    - Proprietary technology naming (Enzymedica's Thera-blend)
    - Concrete equivalence claims (NOW: "1 capsule = 1 cup of milk")
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## Email Copy Patterns

```
EMAIL COPY PATTERNS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SUBJECT LINE FORMULAS
  Formula 1: "Curiosity Gap + Personal Pronoun"
    Examples: "Come for the convenience, stay for the benefits" (AG1),
              "Why we made a powder (not a pill)" (DairyAssure planned)
  Formula 2: "Direct Benefit Claim"
    Examples: "Whole body health for less than $3/day" (AG1),
              "Raise your probiotic standards" (Seed)
  Formula 3: "Urgency + Exclusivity"
    Examples: "This Exclusive Welcome Kit Offer Ends Tonight!" (AG1)

WELCOME SERIES ARCHITECTURE
  Consensus sequence (from AG1, Seed, Arrae, Ritual, OLLY):
    Email 1: Welcome + deliver offer/value (immediate)
    Email 2: Education — how the product works (Day 1-2)
    Email 3: Social proof — reviews, results, celebrity (Day 3-5)
    Email 4: Trust & transparency — ingredients, sourcing, science (Day 5-9)
    Email 5: Soft close — offer reminder + urgency (Day 7-14)
  Optimal length: 5 emails over 7-14 days
  Discount strategy:
    Premium brands (AG1, Seed): No % discount. Free gift, welcome kit,
                                subscription savings framing.
    Mid-market (DairyAssure tier): 10% first order is standard.

ABANDONMENT BEST PRACTICES
  Timing: Email 1 at 30-60 min, Email 2 at 24hr, Email 3 at 48-72hr
  Email count: 3 (consensus)
  Escalation: Reminder only → free shipping → 10% discount
  Key stat: ~70% of carts abandoned. Recovery drops to ~5% after 72hr.

POST-PURCHASE PATTERNS
  Review ask timing: 14-21 days (supplements need adequate experience time)
  Replenishment: 20-25 days for 30-day supply products
  Sequence: Order confirm → shipping → usage education (Day 3-5) →
            check-in (Day 7-10) → review ask (Day 14-21) →
            replenishment (Day 20-25)

COPY VOICE IN THIS CATEGORY
  What the category defaults to: Clinical/medical ("enzyme supplement,"
                                 "lactase units," "digestive support")
  What the winners do differently: Conversational permission-giving
                                   ("eat what you love," "enjoy dairy,"
                                   "have your cake and digest it too")
  White space opportunity: Confident warmth with a touch of humor —
                           nobody in lactase owns a voice that makes you
                           SMILE about solving this problem
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## Key Competitive Insights

1. **Format is the moat.** No competitor sells single-ingredient lactase powder in stick packs. Enzymedica, NOW, and Lactaid all use pills/capsules. Seeking Health uses liquid drops (24hr pre-treatment). DairyAssure's sprinkle-on-food format is genuinely novel and must be the hero of every page.

2. **Multi-enzyme competitors position purity as a weakness.** Enzymedica's "Lacto" and NOW's "Dairy Digest Complete" both sell lactase + protease + lipase combos and frame "complete digestion" as superior. DairyAssure must reframe single-ingredient as intentional purity, not a gap: "One ingredient because that's all you need."

3. **"Doctor recommended" is the most underleveraged asset.** Seeking Health and Hilma build their entire trust architecture around doctor authority. DairyAssure mentions it once in the announcement bar. This should be a pillar.

4. **Speed-to-relief is a category differentiator.** Arrae claims "under 1 hour." DairyAssure claims "90+ minutes of effectiveness" — this is actually a duration claim, not a speed claim. Need both: "Works in minutes. Lasts 90+."

5. **Enzymedica's "DairyAssist" product** has a confusingly similar name to DairyAssure — worth monitoring for brand confusion.

6. **Every winning brand has a guarantee near the buy button.** Ridge (99-day), AG1 (90-day), Enzymedica (45-day). DairyAssure has "Money-Back Guarantee" in the hero but nothing on the PDP.
