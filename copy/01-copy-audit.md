## Summary

**Overall Score: 2.9 / 5.0**

The homepage is strong — it has a well-crafted narrative flow from empathy to product to proof to action, with consistent brand voice throughout. The copy hits the right emotional notes ("Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama") and the section architecture (empathy → product intro → how it works → comparison → trust → buy → guarantee → story → FAQ → reviews) follows proven DTC conversion patterns.

**The critical problem is everywhere else.** The PDP has 4 empty collapsible tabs, 2 sections with raw Dawn placeholder text, and zero trust copy near the buy button. The cart has no conversion optimization. The footer, contact page, 404, and search all use generic Dawn defaults with no brand voice.

**Top 5 Gaps (P0-P1):**
1. PDP has Dawn placeholder copy and empty content tabs — professional credibility killer
2. PDP buy area has zero trust signals (no guarantee, no reviews, no shipping info)
3. Cart has no free shipping progress bar despite $50 threshold being a key selling point
4. Cart and footer use generic Dawn copy — brand voice drops off a cliff after homepage
5. No "Doctor recommended" content beyond a single announcement bar line

**What Phase 2 (Competitive Research) should focus on:**
- How top supplement DTC brands (AG1, Seed, Ritual) handle PDP copy structure
- Cart conversion copy patterns (shipping thresholds, trust badges, upsells)
- Authority/credibility section patterns ("Doctor recommended" execution)
- Segment-specific messaging approaches for lactose intolerance audiences
- Footer and email capture incentive strategies
