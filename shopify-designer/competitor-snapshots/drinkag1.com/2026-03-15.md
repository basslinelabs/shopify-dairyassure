| Flavor accents | Orange `#FF942A`, Magenta `#D057AB`, Gold `#E3BE38` | Flavor variant indicators |
| Light bg | Off-white / warm stone | Risk reversal section, alternating sections |

**Key pattern:** Alternating dark (teal/black) and light (white/off-white) sections create visual rhythm and prevent monotony on a long single-product page.

### Typography

- **Headings:** Modern sans-serif, bold weight, large scale (estimated 32-48px desktop)
- **Body:** Same sans-serif family, regular weight, comfortable reading size
- **Hierarchy via weight:** Bold vs regular within the same font family, not font switching
- **No serif fonts** -- fully modern/clinical aesthetic

### Layout Approaches

- **Full-bleed hero** with contained text overlay
- **Split layouts** (50/50) for product benefits and offer sections
- **3-column grids** for How It Works, trust badges
- **Horizontal carousels** for ingredients, testimonials, reviews, blog (4 separate carousels)
- **Generous vertical spacing:** 60-100px+ between sections
- **Content max-width:** Contained center column (~1200px) within full-bleed backgrounds

### Photography Style

- Professional celebrity/athlete portraits (headshots and action)
- Clean product beauty shots with controlled lighting
- Ingredient close-up photography (real foods: apple, citrus, bilberry)
- Lifestyle "in-use" imagery (mixing, drinking)
- High saturation, well-lit, aspirational

### Overall Aesthetic

**Premium wellness meets clinical credibility.** Clean, modern, generous whitespace. Not playful or whimsical -- it is serious about health but approachable through celebrity/lifestyle framing. The dark teal anchors everything in a "nature + science" positioning.

## 4. Single-SKU Brand Homepage Strategy

AG1 solves the "one product, long homepage" problem through **narrative layering**:

### Variety Without SKU Proliferation

1. **Carousel rotation** -- 4 hero slides make one product feel like a campaign, not a product page
2. **Flavor embedding** -- 4 flavors (Original, Citrus, Berry, Tropical) create perceived variety
3. **Ingredient decomposition** -- Breaking 75+ ingredients into 5 category cards (Vitamins, Probiotics, Adaptogens, Antioxidants, Superfoods) makes one product feel like many
4. **Use-case segmentation** -- "It's a match if you..." positions one product for 5 different customer profiles
5. **Complementary product** -- AGZ sleep formula as cross-sell prevents single-product fatigue
6. **Welcome kit bundling** -- Canister + scoop + shaker + sample pack makes "one product" feel like a curated kit

### Product Presentation Contexts

The same product appears in completely different visual contexts throughout the page:
- Celebrity lifestyle (Hugh Jackman's morning)
- Clinical/scientific (data charts, ingredient close-ups)
- Step-by-step ritual (How It Works)
- Value comparison (vs. buying 6 separate supplements)
- Unboxing/gifting (Welcome Kit)

### Key Takeaway for DairyAssure

AG1 never shows a product grid or "shop" layout on the homepage. The entire page is a single conversion funnel for one subscription. Every section exists to move the visitor closer to "Get Started."
