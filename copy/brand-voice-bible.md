## Voice Attributes

Derived from Hawke Media brand character guidelines:

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|------------|
| **Clear** — We say exactly what we mean. No jargon, no hedging. "One ingredient. That's all you need." | **Clinical** — We never sound like a supplement facts panel or a doctor's note. |
| **Conversational** — We talk like a friend across the table, not a brand on a billboard. | **Overly friendly** — We don't try too hard. No forced enthusiasm or exclamation-mark overload. |
| **Honest** — We're transparent about what's in it (one ingredient) and what's not. We earn trust through simplicity. | **Blunt** — We're never dismissive of someone's discomfort or concerns. We take the problem seriously, then make the solution feel easy. |
| **Encouraging** — We celebrate the return to dairy. We make people feel smart for choosing simplicity. | **Preachy** — We never lecture about health, ingredients, or why pills are bad. We state facts and let people decide. |
| **Simple** — Our product is simple. Our language matches. Short sentences, real words, no complexity theater. | **Shallow** — Simple doesn't mean we lack substance. We can go deep on the science when asked (FAQ, product tabs). |

## Sentence Patterns

- **Headlines:** Short. Punchy. Max 8 words. A statement, not a question. Period at end for finality.
  - Pattern: `[Outcome/Promise]. [Full stop.]`
  - Examples: "Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama." / "One Ingredient. Zero Compromise." / "Welcome Back to Dairy."

- **Subheadlines:** One sentence that expands the headline with a specific benefit and differentiator.
  - Pattern: `[Product description] + [key differentiator] + [what you DON'T have to do]`
  - Example: "A single-ingredient lactase powder you sprinkle on any food or drink. No pills. No planning. No compromise."

- **Body copy:** 2-3 short sentences per block. Lead with the benefit. End with proof or a grounding detail.
  - Pattern: `[Benefit]. [How it works]. [Proof/detail].`
  - Example: "DairyAssure provides 90+ minutes of relief — enough to last through your entire meal and beyond. Just sprinkle and eat."

- **CTAs:** Action verb + specific outcome. Personal > generic. Concrete > abstract.
  - Strong: "TRY DAIRYASSURE TODAY" / "GET MY STICKS" / "ENJOY DAIRY AGAIN"
  - Avoid: "Shop Now" / "Buy" / "Add to Cart" (when possible — buy button itself is fine)

- **Trust copy:** Fact-first fragments. No exclamation marks. Checkmarks or pipes as separators.
  - Pattern: `[Certification/fact] | [Certification/fact] | [Certification/fact]`
  - Example: "Single Ingredient | Kosher | Non-GMO | Made in USA"

## Words We Never Use

| Banned Word/Phrase | Why | Use Instead |
|-------------------|-----|-------------|
| Suffer / suffering | Pathologizes a common condition | "deal with" / "experience" |
| Cure / treat / heal | Medical claims we can't make (FDA) | "support" / "help" / "relief" |
| Supplement (in headlines) | Too clinical, sounds like vitamins aisle | "lactase powder" / "enzyme" |
| Bloating / gas / diarrhea | Too graphic for marketing copy — people know | "discomfort" / "the drama" |
| Clinically proven | We don't have our own clinical trials | "Doctor recommended" |
| Revolutionary / breakthrough | Hyperbolic, triggers skepticism. We are NOT preachy. | "Simple" / "Pure" / "Just" |
| Hack / biohack | Wrong audience, wrong category | "Solution" / "answer" |
| All-natural | Legally meaningless, overused | "Single ingredient" / "Pure" |
| Cheap / affordable (in headlines) | Devalues the product (but accessibility IS a value) | "Worth it" / "accessible" / frame daily cost |
| Pill alternative | Defines us by what we're NOT | "Lactase powder" (what we ARE) |
| Tummy / belly | Too cutesy — we are conversational, not overly friendly | "stomach" / "gut" / "comfort" |
| Intolerance (in headlines) | Clinical label, slightly negative | "sensitivity" or just "dairy" |
| Amazing! / Incredible! / OMG! | We are NOT overly friendly. Calm confidence, not hype. | State facts plainly. |

## Voice Guardrails

1. **Always lead with the food moment, not the condition.** Say "enjoy pizza night" before "manage lactose intolerance." We sell the joy (Taste Joy), not the diagnosis.

2. **Never apologize for simplicity.** "Just one ingredient" is a feature, not a limitation. Simple Solutions Only.

3. **Keep copy scannable.** No paragraph longer than 3 sentences on any product or marketing page. We are Clear and Simple.

4. **Proof follows emotion.** Hook with the feeling ("Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama"), then back it with facts ("Single ingredient. Kosher. Non-GMO. Made in USA."). Never lead with the facts.

5. **Every page should feel like the homepage.** Brand voice doesn't stop at the hero section. Cart, 404, empty states, footer — if a customer reads it, it should sound like DairyAssure. Your Best Backup shows up everywhere.

6. **Empathy before solution.** Acknowledge the experience ("we understand the awkward date moments, the declined desserts") before presenting the product. We're Encouraging, not Preachy.

7. **Accessibility is a value, not a discount.** We're cost conscious and believe everyone should have access. Frame affordability as a mission, not a sale.

8. **The sugar packet vision.** Our long-term positioning is to be as essential and recognizable as the sugar packet — present at every table. Copy should feel everyday-essential, not premium-exclusive.
