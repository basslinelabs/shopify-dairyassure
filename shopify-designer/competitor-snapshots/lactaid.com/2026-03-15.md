## 2. Visual Design Patterns

### Colors (observed from screenshot and markup)

| Role | Color | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary brand** | Dark navy blue | Used for header bar, nav, CTA buttons, headings |
| **Background** | White (#FFFFFF) | Dominant page background |
| **Accent background** | Light gray / off-white | Alternating section backgrounds |
| **CTA buttons** | Dark navy blue bg, white text | Consistent throughout page |
| **Text links** | Dark navy blue, underlined | Secondary CTAs styled as text links |
| **Category labels** | Uppercase, navy text | "DESSERTS", "BREAKFAST", etc. |

### Typography

- **Headings:** Sans-serif, bold weight. Mix of sentence case ("More than Just Milk") and ALL CAPS for CTAs/labels
- **Body copy:** Sans-serif, regular weight, comfortable line-height
- **CTA buttons:** ALL CAPS, medium weight, sans-serif
- **Product category labels:** ALL CAPS under product carousel images
- **Recipe card labels:** ALL CAPS category, then Title Case for recipe name

### Layout Approaches

- **Split layouts:** Text + image side-by-side blocks (Brand Story, Product Spotlights)
- **Carousels:** Horizontal scrolling carousels for products (10 items), recipes (5 items), articles (7 items), buy-now products (8 items) -- carousels are the dominant pattern
- **Card-based grids:** Recipe cards and article cards with image + category label + title + metadata
- **Full-width hero:** Image + overlaid text with CTA
- **No pricing shown:** Products are presented by category with images, no prices on the homepage

## 3. Hero Section Treatment

- **Headline:** "More than Just Milk" (H1)
- **Sub-copy:** "We've got a line of lactose-free dairy products so you can enjoy all of the goodness of real milk and dairy, just without the discomfort!"
- **CTA:** "EXPLORE OUR PRODUCTS" (single CTA, all-caps, navy button)
- **Imagery:** Lifestyle photography — coffee pour scene with Lactaid milk carton. Warm, inviting, food-centric (not product-on-white).
- **Layout:** Text block positioned above/alongside the image (on mobile, text stacks above image)
- **No video:** Static image only in hero
- **No pricing or product shot:** Hero is aspirational/lifestyle, not transactional

### Key takeaway for DairyAssure
Lactaid leads with a broad category message ("More than Just Milk") because they have many products. A single-SKU brand like DairyAssure should lead with a more specific, benefit-driven hero.

### Product Spotlights (Sections 6-7)

- Copy emphasizes newness ("New Limited Edition"), specific benefits ("10g of protein per serving"), and sensory appeal ("extra creamy and wonderfully rich")
- Each has a dedicated CTA linking to the product category

### Buy Now Section (Section 12)
- 8 individual products listed with:
  - Product packshot image
  - Full product name (e.g., "LACTAID(R) Lactose-Free Whole Milk")
  - "BUY NOW" button (links to retailer/store locator, NOT a cart — Lactaid does not sell DTC)
- **No pricing displayed** — because Lactaid is sold through retail channels, not DTC
- **No reviews/ratings** shown on product cards

### Key takeaway for DairyAssure
Lactaid cannot sell directly. Their "BUY NOW" sends users to retailers. DairyAssure has a DTC advantage — should lean into add-to-cart, pricing transparency, and subscription options that Lactaid cannot offer.

## 5. Social Proof / Trust Signals

### What's present
- **Farmer Dave imagery:** A farmer with a cow and glass of milk — authenticity signal for sourcing
- **"No artificial growth hormones" claim:** Prominent in brand story section with asterisk/disclaimer
- **"100% real milk" messaging:** Repeated throughout
- **Recipe content:** Positions brand as a cooking/lifestyle enabler (implicit trust through utility)
- **Instagram social integration:** "FOLLOW @LACTAID ON INSTAGRAM" — social proof through community

### What's notably ABSENT
- **No star ratings or review counts** anywhere on homepage
- **No testimonials or customer quotes**
- **No "doctor recommended" or clinical claims**
- **No certification badges** (Non-GMO, Kosher, etc. not displayed on homepage)
- **No press logos** ("As seen in...")
- **No numerical social proof** (e.g., "X million servings sold")

### Key takeaway for DairyAssure
Lactaid relies on brand recognition built over decades rather than explicit trust signals. DairyAssure as a challenger brand MUST compensate with stronger social proof: reviews, doctor endorsements, certifications, and clinical claims.

- **Hero stacks vertically:** Headline + copy + CTA above the hero image

## 8. Content Strategy Analysis

### Content Pillars on Homepage
1. **Products** (~30% of page) — hero, product carousel, product spotlights, buy now section
2. **Recipes** (~20%) — recipe carousel, substitution guide, Instagram
3. **Education** (~20%) — lactose sensitivity articles carousel
4. **Brand story** (~10%) — Farmer Dave section
5. **Commerce** (~10%) — buy now section, coupons link
6. **Email capture** (~10%) — newsletter signup

### Voice & Tone
- **Friendly and approachable** — "We've got a line of..." "just without the discomfort!"
- **Reassuring** — "100% real milk", "real delicious", repeated emphasis that lactose-free is still "real"
- **Not clinical** — avoids medical/scientific language entirely
- **Food-forward** — emphasis on enjoyment, recipes, flavors rather than symptoms or conditions
- **Inclusive** — positioned as a food brand for everyone, not a medical product for a condition

### Messaging Hierarchy
1. We are REAL dairy (not a substitute/alternative)
2. You can ENJOY food without worry
3. We have MANY products for every occasion
4. Here are RECIPES to inspire you
5. Learn about your SENSITIVITY (educational, not clinical)
6. Find us at a store NEAR YOU

## 9. Competitive Observations for DairyAssure

### Lactaid's Strengths
- Massive product portfolio creates "one brand for everything" positioning
- Recipe content drives repeat visits and SEO
- Lifestyle photography feels aspirational, not medicinal
- Education content builds authority on lactose sensitivity

### Lactaid's Weaknesses (DairyAssure Opportunities)
- **No DTC capability** — cannot sell direct, no subscription, no cart
- **No social proof on homepage** — zero reviews, ratings, or testimonials
- **No clinical/science credibility** — no doctor endorsements, no study citations
- **No urgency or conversion optimization** — passive browsing experience
- **Supplement product buried** — "Dietary Supplements" is last (10th) in their product carousel
- **No differentiation of supplement** — the Fast Act supplement is treated as just another SKU in a dairy product lineup, not a hero product
- **No personalization** — no quiz, no "which product is right for you"
- **Generic messaging** — "More than Just Milk" is brand-level, not benefit-specific
- **No free shipping messaging** — no commerce incentives on page
- **No mobile-optimized conversion** — no sticky add-to-cart, no quick-buy

### DairyAssure Strategic Advantages to Emphasize
1. **Single-product focus** — can tell a deeper, more compelling story about one product
2. **DTC model** — add-to-cart, subscribe & save, free shipping threshold
3. **Doctor recommended** — clinical credibility Lactaid completely lacks
4. **Fast-acting benefit** — specific mechanism of action messaging
5. **Portability (stick packs)** — grab-and-go convenience Lactaid supplements don't emphasize
6. **Review/testimonial integration** — social proof Lactaid ignores
7. **Single ingredient / clean label** — purity messaging vs. Lactaid's corporate feel
