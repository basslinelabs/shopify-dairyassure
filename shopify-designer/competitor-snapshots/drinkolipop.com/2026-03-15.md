## 2. Visual Design Patterns

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary brand | Hunter Green | `#14433D` |
| Background | White | `#FFFFFF` |
| Body text | Dark Gray | `#3A3A3A` |
| Borders/dividers | Light Gray | `#EBEBEB` |

| Sale/promo text | Coral Red | `#FA4545` |
| Card borders | Sage Gray | `#C4D0CF` |
| Supporting | Soft pastels, cream tones in wave graphics |

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** Olipop uses a single dominant brand color (hunter green) with neutral backgrounds. DairyAssure's periwinkle/indigo palette is more distinctive — lean into that differentiation rather than mimicking the green-health trope.

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Headlines | WindsorEF | 900 (ultra-bold) |
| Body / UI | Helvetica/Arial stack | 400, 700 |
| Logotype | Custom "Ano" | — |

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** WindsorEF is a rounded serif that reads as warm and retro — it softens the health messaging. DairyAssure uses Helvetica Neue across the board. Consider whether a display font for headlines (something warm, not clinical) would strengthen "Your Best Backup" personality.

### Layout Grid

- 12-column responsive grid
- Desktop gutters: 55px / Mobile gutters: 22px
- Breakpoints: 750px, 990px, 1400px+
- Section vertical rhythm: 35-55px spacing
- Full-width sections with max-width content containers

### Distinctive Visual Elements

- **SVG wave dividers** between sections (organic, soda-bottle-inspired curves)
- **CGI product renders** on transparent backgrounds (not lifestyle photography on homepage)
- **Flavor-specific color theming** via CSS custom properties — each flavor gets its own color treatment
- **Minimal border-radius** on cards (10px), generous on tooltips (15px)

## 3. Hero Section Treatment

**Current hero:** Campaign-driven (Flavor Face-Off bracket competition), not a permanent brand hero.

**Structure:**
- Full-bleed background image (responsive: separate mobile/tablet/desktop assets)
- Center-aligned text overlay
- Headline + subheadline + CTA button + secondary incentive line
- No product image in hero — purely campaign/brand imagery

**Transitions:** Responsive hero heights (640px mobile, 659px desktop)

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** Olipop rotates their hero for campaigns rather than using a static brand statement. For a single-product brand like DairyAssure, a product-hero hybrid (product image + "Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama" + CTA) is more appropriate, but consider seasonal/campaign hero swaps as the brand matures.

## 4. Product Presentation Approach

**Strategy: Flavor-first, health-second.**

Each product card contains:
- Product image (CGI render, clean background)
- Hover state: alternate image (lifestyle/collection shot)
- Product name (e.g., "Vintage Cola")
- One-line taste-focused tagline (e.g., "Old-fashioned taste meets new-fashioned ingredients")
- "+ Add 12 Pack" — direct add-to-cart, not "Learn More"

**No prices visible on homepage cards.** The focus is on flavor appeal and frictionless add-to-cart.

**No ingredient callouts on homepage.** Health info is deferred to dedicated Learn pages.

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** DairyAssure has only 2 SKUs (10K and 20K FCC), so the product grid approach doesn't apply. But the principle of leading with the experience ("Enjoy dairy without the drama") over the mechanism ("lactase enzyme powder") is directly transferable. Keep ingredient/science details on the PDP, not the homepage.

## 5. Social Proof & Trust Signals

**Notably minimal on the homepage:**
- No customer reviews or star ratings displayed
- No testimonial quotes
- No "As Seen In" press logos
- No influencer content
- No user-generated content
- No customer count ("Join 500K+ happy guts")

**Trust signals present (indirect):**
- "Satisfaction Guarantee" in footer
- "Registered Dietitian Program" linked
- Ingredients transparency page
- Multiple payment options (Apple Pay, Shop Pay)

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** Olipop can afford sparse social proof because they have massive brand awareness (retail distribution, celebrity investors). DairyAssure, as a newer DTC brand, needs social proof more aggressively on the homepage — doctor recommendations, customer testimonials, review counts. The "Doctor recommended, dessert approved" angle is a significant trust advantage that Olipop doesn't deploy on their homepage.

- "30% off subscriptions when you resubscribe with code 30RESUB"

### CTA Visual Treatment
- Primary buttons: Dark background (#3A3A3A), white text, minimal padding

**Takeaway for DairyAssure:** Olipop's CTA strategy is aggressive on subscription conversion (multiple discount codes, prominent "Subscribe & Save" nav item). DairyAssure should consider: (1) a persistent announcement bar with free shipping threshold ($50), (2) direct add-to-cart on homepage product sections, (3) subscription as a primary nav item if/when subscription is launched.

## 7. Brand Personality in Design: Playful vs. Clinical Spectrum

### Where Olipop sits: Playful-nostalgic with functional credibility

**Playful signals:**
- Flavor Face-Off bracket game (gamification)
- SpongeBob collaboration (pop culture)
- Flavor names: "Vintage Cola," "Ridge Rush," "Pineapple Paradise"
- Taglines: "So crisp, so refreshing," "Better than dessert," "Really cherry"
- Wave graphics (organic, fun shapes)
- WindsorEF headline font (warm, retro)

**Functional/credibility signals:**
- "A New Kind of Soda" (category disruption framing)
- "High Fiber. Less Sugar. Delicious Flavors." (benefit-led)
- Digestive Health + Ingredients pages in navigation
- Registered Dietitian Program
- Clean, uncluttered layout (not juvenile despite playfulness)

**Balance approach:** Health benefits are stated once, clearly, then the rest of the page is about flavor and fun. They don't repeat the health message. The design does the credibility work (clean layout, professional photography) while the copy does the personality work (playful names, casual tone).

### Relevance to DairyAssure's "Your Best Backup" personality

DairyAssure's brand personality is "reliable friend who's got your back" — warm, reassuring, slightly humorous. Olipop's approach suggests:

1. **State the functional benefit once, boldly, then move on.** Don't hammer "lactase enzyme" repeatedly. Say "Enjoy Dairy Without the Drama" and let the rest of the page be about the experience.
2. **Let design convey trust; let copy convey personality.** Clean layouts = credible. Playful copy = approachable.
3. **Avoid clinical aesthetics.** Olipop never shows lab coats, microscopes, or digestive diagrams on their homepage despite being a gut-health product. DairyAssure should similarly avoid clinical imagery.
4. **Gamification and community can replace traditional social proof** at scale, but DairyAssure isn't there yet — prioritize testimonials and doctor endorsements first.

## 8. Key Design Patterns to Consider for DairyAssure

### Adopt
- **Announcement bar** with rotating promos / free shipping threshold
- **Lean homepage** — don't over-stuff. 4-5 strong sections beat 10 mediocre ones.
- **Flavor/experience-first product framing** (adapt to "occasion-first" for DairyAssure: pizza night, ice cream date, travel)
- **Direct add-to-cart** on homepage product sections
- **Responsive hero images** (separate mobile/desktop assets for optimal framing)

### Adapt
- **Product carousel** becomes a "Which strength is right for you?" comparison for DairyAssure's 2 SKUs
- **Wave dividers** could translate to softer section transitions (not hard lines) that reinforce DairyAssure's approachable brand
- **Campaign hero rotation** — save for later when DairyAssure has seasonal campaigns

### Avoid
- **Sparse social proof** — Olipop can get away with it; DairyAssure cannot yet
- **No pricing on homepage** — for a single-product DTC brand, being transparent about price on the homepage reduces friction
- **Heavy discount code strategy** — devalues a premium positioning; DairyAssure should lead with value, not discounts
